About

Artistic Roots

Nancy Lee is a Filipino-Chinese contemporary and mixed-media artist born and raised in the Philippines. Inspired by her grandfather, a Fine Arts professor and professional watercolorist, Nancy developed a passion for art at the early age of three. During her formative years, she explored various media, including watercolor, oil pastels, ink, and colored pencils, laying the foundation for her artistic journey.

Determined to refine her techniques, Nancy pursued a Fine Arts degree, majoring in Painting, while also exploring Interior Design. However, her true calling remained in painting, leading her to fully embrace it as she nears the completion of her studies.

Community Engagement and Achievements

Throughout her college years, Nancy actively contributed to the art community by conducting summer workshops for children and working as a part-time illustrator for children's books. Her dedication to her craft has also driven her to participate in prestigious art competitions such as the Shell National Art Competition, Metrobank Art & Design Excellence (MADE), and Sining Filipinas by Zonta.

Since 2020, Nancy has been an active participant in numerous group exhibitions, showcasing her acrylic and mixed-media artworks. A significant milestone in her artistic career was achieving her 100th sold artwork in September 2024, a testament to her growing impact in the art scene and her expanding network of patrons and collectors. Her works can be found in a variety of spaces, including clinics, residential homes, and commercial establishments.

Beyond her studio practice, Nancy is a passionate advocate for animal welfare, using her art to support nonprofit organizations dedicated to rescuing and rehabilitating strays. She firmly believes in the transformative power of art, inspiring even non-artists to embrace creativity in their daily lives.
